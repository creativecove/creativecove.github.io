The functional difference between HEX, RGB, and HSL is as follows: HEX uses 3 hexadecimals to determin the red, green, and blue. RGB uses standard numerical numbers ranging from 0 to 255 to determin the amount of red, green, and blue. HSL uses the hue for the color, the saturation for amount of color, and lightness for the amount of light being given.

Preferred color code format: HEX

HWB, or hue whiteness blackness, is another format to do colors, functioning most similarly to HSL or CMYK. It intakes the hue for the color, then how bright the color will be, then how dark. The main difference is how the birghtness and darkness needs to add up to 100%.

CMYK is another format to fo colors, functioning similarly to HSL or HWB. It intakes Cyan, Megenta, Yellow, and Black to make different colors.
